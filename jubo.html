Supporting Long-term Healthcare Institute using Health Data

Background: This project was completed during my Internship with Jubo Health. Through this project I have gained a deeper understanding of how long-term care institutes operates and how I could make use of my skills to support them. I joined the project to develop a prototype for the enhancement of jubo’s Nursing Information System. The aim was to create a data visualization dashboard embedded in NIS that could represent clients’ current and past health status from multiple domains of input.

The project was separated into two phases:
- Cooperated with Jubo’s design team, customer success team, product team, data team to discuss the concept of what and how to be present in the dashboard. Conduct data analysis from MongoDB and implement the discussed concept through data visualization.
- Modified design concepts through user interviews (physician, nurse, nurse aide, nutritionist). Completed ETL process to hourly update the dashboard, and make the dashboard into a interactable web application.

My role(s): Developer, Researcher
Collaborator(s): Sean Chiang (Data Manager), Yahan Chou (designer)
Methods(s): Contextual Inquiry Questionnaire Usability Testing Focus Group Prototyping
Skill(s): Python NoSQL BigQuery Airflow Tableau

Motivation: The existing system for healthcare professionals is imperfect, long-term care institutions are filled with paperwork, therefore, decreasing the efficacy in coworking with different functionalities. There is also a lack of complete system to track individual health history. This project aims to provide a solution to the existing issue.

Findings through Questionnaire

The top 5 most desirable features within an individual health monitor dashboard is:

  • · Vital Signs
  • · Weight/BMI
  • · Regular Assessments
  • · Blood Sugar/Insulin consumption
  • · Drug Intake

How healthcare professionals plan to use the dashboard:

  • · Actively checking the health trends of cases
  • · References when formulating a personalized care plan
  • · Information provided to physicians when seeking medical treatment
  • · Actively checking when system reminds of something abnormal
  • · Case discussion during regular evaluation
  • · Use it as a tool when communicating with family members

Feedbacks:

  • · An overview of the various health data from patients can be integrated into the care of residents. For cross-professional collaboration, the dashboard can be used by nurses, nutritionists, social workers, occupational therapists, and physical therapists to customize specialized care plans for residents.
  • · An overview of health information can serve as a standard for physicians to see. Some hospitals are adopting medical visits through zoom. The dashboard can provide patients' health condition alterations, resulting in better communication. It is especially useful in cases of psychiatric patients, who may not be able to convey their condition through words.
